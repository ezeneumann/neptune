Emerging Themes & Trends

 

  • Convenience and Frictionless Experiences: Consumers and corporate buyers alike now expect not just digital options, but streamlined, guided experiences—spurring investment in automation, self-service, and AI-driven personalization.
  • Purposeful Innovation: Innovation is increasingly pragmatic, with modular launches and fast feedback cycles supplanting big-bang product rollouts; brands are co-creating with partners to de-risk ideas and validate demand earlier.
  • Sustainability as Competitive Currency: Sustainability claims tied to packaging or core product attributes are driving shopper attention, especially among under-40 consumers, but only where clearly linked to differentiating benefits.
  • Returning to Familiar Ground: After several years of disruption and experimental behavior, there is a gradual return to baseline choices—favoring trusted formats and known brands—while openness to value-led 'newstalgia' innovation persists.
  • Corporate Spend Scrutiny: B2B and institutional buyers are exhibiting tighter control on budgets, leading to slower but more deliberate adoption of ancillary services and tech upgrades.
